From Skyscrapers to Stillness

I spent 15 years working as a civil/structural engineer in Canada, helping design buildings and structures to withstand wind, time, and uncertainty. I earned a PhD, worked on international projects, and built a career that looked solid from the outside.

Then I traveled to India to sit in silence for ten days at a time, practicing Vipassana meditation. Later, life brought me to Switzerland, where I became a father and slowly began redesigning not just structures, but my idea of success.

I spent years helping buildings withstand the wind.
Now I’m learning how to sit still in it.

Kindergarten of Wisdom is where I share what I’m discovering along the way.

Who this is for

  • You’ve achieved things on paper, but feel a quiet dissatisfaction you can’t ignore anymore.
  • You’re curious about meditation, yoga, or inner work, but allergic to dogma and spiritual clichés.
  • You want to bring more presence into work, parenting, relationships, and technology.
  • You’d rather think deeply than chase quick hacks.
